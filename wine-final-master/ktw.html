Kindzmarauli is one of the most well-known Georgian wines given its popularity in the Soviet period. Kindzmarauli is sweet but not too sweet because it is made from a tannic and complex grape varietal, Saperavi. The characteristics of the Saperavi grape lend balance to the sugar resulting in a well structured wine great for pairings.

Dry red wine is made from the pitcher of the Otskhani Saperian grape variety, grown in Imereti, western Georgia. The wine has a dark pomegranate color, characterized by aromas of rosemary, berries and spices. Tastes a lively, bodily taste, with an acidity and a long finish. The crop is harvested grain-by-grain. Taste at + 16-18 ° with a spicy dish, well blended with meat, raw herbs and vegetables.

Tsinandali is delightful and refreshing white wine. It is blend of 80% Rkatsiteli (pronounced Ra-ka-see-tell-ee) and 20% Mtsvane (pronounced Ma-tsvah-nay). The grapes are sourced from the Tsinandali micro-zone in the Kakheti appellation and must be this blend to receive the name Tsinandali. This wine has been a consistent favorite in the US having been featured in Wine Spectator, the Today Show and the Washington Post.
