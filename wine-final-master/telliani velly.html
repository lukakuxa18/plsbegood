Kindzmarauli (Kinz-mah-rah-oo-lee) is one of the most well-known Georgian wines given its popularity in the Soviet period. Many American wine-drinkers are put off by the word "sweet" but this authentic naturally semi-sweet wine has been making American converts for the last decade. Kindzmarauli is sweet but not too sweet because it is made from a tannic and complex grape varietal, Saperavi. The characteristics of the Saperavi grape lend balance to the sugar resulting in a well structured wine great for pairings.

Tsinandali is delightful and refreshing white wine. It is blend of 80% Rkatsiteli (pronounced Ra-ka-see-tell-ee) and 20% Mtsvane (pronounced Ma-tsvah-nay). The grapes are sourced from the Tsinandali micro-zone in the Kakheti appellation and must be this blend to receive the name Tsinandali. This wine has been a consistent favorite in the US having been featured in Wine Spectator, the Today Show and the Washington Post.

Tvishi is a semi-sweet wine produced from Tsolikouri grapes sourced from the Tvish viticulture district in Lechkhumi located high in the Caucasus mountains. Tvishi is a naturally semi-sweet wine (fermentation stopped by temperature control) made in stainless steel. Tvishi is closer to a Riesling in it sugar content, acidity and flavor profile.
